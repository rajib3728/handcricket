Rules of the Game

1. The game is played between two players, the user and the bot (or another human).

2. The game starts with a coin toss to determine who will bat first. The user can choose heads (H) or tails (T).

3. The player who wins the toss can choose to either bat or bowl first.

4. The batting player will choose a number between 1 and 6, representing the runs they want to score.

5. The bowling player will also choose a number between 1 and 6, representing the runs they want to concede.

6. If the batting player's chosen number matches the bowling player's chosen number, the batting player is out.

7. If the batting player's chosen number does not match the bowling player's chosen number, the batting player scores runs equal to the chosen number.

8. The game continues until the batting player is out or reaches a predetermined score limit.

9. The player with the highest score at the end of the game wins.

10. In the case of a tie, the game can be decided by a tiebreaker, such as a super over or a coin toss.
